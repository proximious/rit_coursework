What is the difference between combinational circuits and sequential circuits?

Combinational circuits use logic in present time, where sequential circuits use present and past time logic. The basic circuits we looked at in the beginning of the unit were combinational circuits (simple ones like A'C + BC' for example) and are comprised mostly of basic logic gates, whereas sequential circuits would be like the mod-16 binary counter we made for the experiment, relying on an external trigger to allow for a change in each output of the J-K Flip Flops (flip flops are also mostly used for sequential circuits).
